  • The Scent of Time, by Byung-Chul Han. From The Practical Polymath: "The book is a critique of how our culture of time-optimization and productivity is robbing time of its meaning or “scent” as he puts it. In the book, he opposes the “pilgrim” who is in transition towards his objective to the “tourist” who wants to get there as fast as possible. The former's experience is enriched by the journey that offers an opportunity for contemplation while the latter hops from one thing to another without much focus. According to the author, we are increasingly living life like tourists"
